09-04-2026 15:00

The Swiss city of Lugano is home to one of the world’s most advanced supercomputers, called Alps.

This supercomputer contains nearly 11,000 powerful processors, far stronger than a typical laptop. Alps operates sustainably by using cold water from Lake Lugano to cool down the machines, which produce a lot of heat. The water passes through heat exchangers and returns warmer to the lake without harming the environment. Each of the 2,688 computing blades inside has blue and red pipes for liquid cooling to avoid confusion during maintenance.

Alps helps scientists process large datasets to predict global issues such as sea level rise, glacier melting, and extreme weather events. It runs complex AI models that simulate how rising CO2 levels affect the planet. Near Alps is the Contra Dam, where data on heavy rainfall and snow are studied. Alps uses millions of data points and thousands of altitude levels to perform calculations impossible for normal computers, providing critical insight for climate science.

Difficult words: heat exchanger (a device that transfers heat), dataset (a large collection of data), altitude (height above sea level).

## 🔗 表达 / Chunking Expressions

**home to** （是...的所在地）
Used to say where something or someone is located or lives. Common and neutral register.
- This building is home to several famous art galleries.
- Silicon Valley is home to many technology companies.

**cool down** （冷却）
To make something less hot. This is a common phrasal verb used in many contexts, from technology to personal well-being.
- The fan helps to cool down the room.
- It's important to cool down your muscles after exercising.

**process large datasets** （处理大型数据集）
To analyze and work with very big collections of information. This is a more technical phrase common in scientific and computing contexts.
- Researchers use powerful computers to process large datasets of genetic information.
- The company needs to process large datasets to understand customer behavior.

**extreme weather events** （极端天气事件）
Weather conditions that are very severe and unusual, such as hurricanes, heatwaves, or heavy floods. Used in news and scientific discussions about climate change.
