Tim Cook is stepping down as the CEO of Apple after fifteen years. Under his leadership, the company became a four-trillion-dollar giant and achieved huge financial success.

Although he followed a charismatic founder, Cook managed to grow the business significantly. He will stay on as chairman to handle important relations with China and the White House because he has a unique skill in this area. John Ternus will be the new leader of the company starting this year. Ternus previously worked as the head of hardware engineering, and he has many patents for his designs. Experts believe he might be a more hands-on leader for new products compared to Cook.

This change comes at a time when many people ask if Apple can create a new device as successful as the iPhone. One major problem is that Apple fell behind in the race for AI. Other companies are moving faster, and the company must now catch up to stay the leader. The new boss must also manage difficult global politics while trying to release breakthrough technology.

Difficult words: giant (something or someone that is very, very big), charismatic (having a special power to make people like you and listen to you), patent (an official right to be the only person to make or sell a new invention), hands-on (doing something yourself instead of just telling others what to do).

## 🔗 表达 / Chunking Expressions

**stepping down as the CEO** （辞去CEO职务）
This phrase is used to announce that someone is leaving their position as the chief executive officer of a company. It's a formal way to state a resignation from the highest executive role.
- The current CEO is stepping down as the CEO after a decade of service.
- Many expect the founder to eventually step down as the CEO and hand over the reins.

**grow the business** （发展业务）
This refers to increasing the size, revenue, or market share of a company. It's a common goal for leaders and signifies expansion and success.
- The new marketing strategy helped the company to grow the business rapidly.
- Entrepreneurs are always looking for innovative ways to grow the business.

**stay the leader** （保持领先地位）
This phrase means to remain in the most successful or advanced position in a particular field or competition. It implies maintaining a competitive advantage.
- With constant innovation, the company aims to stay the leader in the industry.
- They invested heavily in research and development to stay the leader in technology.

**catch up to** （追赶上）
To reach the same level or standard as someone or something else, especially after being behind. This is often used in competitive contexts.
- The athlete trained hard to catch up to the leading runners.
- The company needs to work faster to catch up to its competitors in AI development.
