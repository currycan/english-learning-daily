# Phase 3 — UI Design Contract

> Visual and interaction contract for the `content/YYYY-MM-DD.md` Markdown output file.
> This phase produces a static Markdown document consumed by learners on iPhone via push
> notification links. "UI" here means the content format, section structure, entry layout,
> and language-mixing rules — not a web component system.
>
> Generated by gsd-ui-researcher. Verified by gsd-ui-checker.

## Spacing Scale

Markdown does not use CSS spacing tokens. The equivalent contract for this phase is
**structural whitespace** — blank lines between elements that control visual rhythm in
rendered Markdown.

| Token | Value | Usage |
|-------|-------|-------|
| xs | 0 blank lines | Between a vocabulary word line and its blockquote example |
| sm | 1 blank line | Between consecutive vocabulary entries; between chunk entries; between Q&A blocks |
| md | 1 blank line | After each section header before first entry |
| lg | 1 blank line | After the last entry in a section before the next `##` header |
| xl | — | Not applicable |
| 2xl | — | Not applicable |
| 3xl | — | Not applicable |

Exceptions: The article body preserves its original paragraph breaks (blank lines between
paragraphs) verbatim — no normalization. The source blockquote line has exactly one blank
line before it and one blank line after it.

## Typography

Markdown heading levels and text roles map to rendered visual hierarchy. Sizes are
governed by the viewer (GitHub / iOS Safari); the contract specifies heading level only.

| Role | Markdown Construct | Weight | Notes |
|------|--------------------|--------|-------|
| Section header | `##` (H2) | Bold (browser default) | Four section headers; emoji prefix required |
| Entry title / key term | `**bold**` inline | Bold | Vocabulary word, chunk phrase, question stem |
| Body prose | Plain paragraph text | Regular | Article body, English definitions, English explanations |
| Example / quote | `>` blockquote | Regular | Vocabulary example sentences (direct article quotes) |
| List item | `- ` unordered list | Regular | Chunk usage examples (2 per chunk) |
| Answer label | `**A (EN):**` / `**A (中):**` inline bold | Bold | Comprehension answer labels |

Source: CONTEXT.md §Markdown 文件结构, §词汇条目格式, §Chunking 表达格式, §阅读理解题格式

## Color

Markdown files carry no color tokens. The color contract here specifies **semantic
highlighting** conveyed through Markdown syntax — not hex values.

| Role | Markdown Mechanism | Usage |
|------|--------------------|-------|
| Dominant (60%) | Plain paragraph text | Article body, English definitions, explanations |
| Secondary (30%) | Blockquote (`>`) | Vocabulary example sentences; source URL attribution |
| Accent (10%) | Bold (`**...**`) | Key terms: vocabulary word + part of speech line; chunk phrase line; question stem |
| Destructive | Not applicable | No destructive actions in this phase |

Accent (`**bold**`) reserved for: vocabulary entry first line, chunk entry first line,
comprehension question stem (`**Q: ...**`), comprehension answer labels (`**A (EN):**`,
`**A (中):**`). Never applied to body prose, explanations, or list items.

Source: CONTEXT.md §词汇条目格式, §Chunking 表达格式, §阅读理解题格式

## Section Structure Contract

Four sections, in this exact order. No section may be omitted or reordered.

| # | Header | Emoji | Content |
|---|--------|-------|---------|
| 1 | `## 📖 文章 / Article` | 📖 | Full article body + source blockquote |
| 2 | `## 📚 词汇 / Vocabulary` | 📚 | 5–8 vocabulary entries |
| 3 | `## 🔗 表达 / Chunking Expressions` | 🔗 | 3–5 chunk entries |
| 4 | `## ❓ 理解 / Comprehension` | ❓ | 3–5 Q&A blocks |

Source: CONTEXT.md §Markdown 文件结构

## Entry Format Contract

### Vocabulary Entry

Exact template (one blank line between consecutive entries):

```
**{word}** ({part_of_speech}) （{chinese_meaning}）— {definition in plain English}.
> "{verbatim example sentence from article}"
```

Rules:
- Definition uses B1-B2 plain English (simpler than the word being defined)
- Example is a direct verbatim quote from the article body — never generated
- Chinese meaning is in Chinese characters
- No blank line between the bold line and its blockquote
- One blank line after the blockquote before the next entry

Source: CONTEXT.md §词汇条目格式, REQUIREMENTS.md AIGEN-01

### Chunking Expression Entry

Exact template (one blank line between consecutive entries):

```
**{chunk expression}** （{chinese_meaning}）
{English explanation of usage context and register.}
- {Example sentence one showing the chunk in use.}
- {Example sentence two showing variation or different context.}
```

Rules:
- Chunk expression and Chinese meaning on the same line
- English explanation on the very next line (no blank line)
- Exactly 2 list items (`-`) for usage examples
- Examples may be generated (not required to be from article) — must be natural and idiomatic
- One blank line after the last list item before the next entry

Source: CONTEXT.md §Chunking 表达格式, REQUIREMENTS.md AIGEN-02

### Comprehension Q&A Block

Exact template (one blank line between consecutive blocks):

```
**Q: {Question in English?}**
**A (EN):** {English answer.}
**A (中):** {中文要点说明。}
```

Rules:
- All three lines are on consecutive lines (no blank lines within a block)
- Question stem is in English
- English answer (`A (EN)`) is in English
- Chinese summary (`A (中)`) is in Chinese
- One blank line after the Chinese answer before the next Q&A block
- At least 1 of the 3–5 questions must be inferential (requires reasoning, not pure recall)

Source: CONTEXT.md §阅读理解题格式, REQUIREMENTS.md AIGEN-03, AIGEN-04

### Article Section Footer

The article body ends with:

```
> Source: {source_url}
```

Rules:
- One blank line between the last paragraph of article body and this line
- One blank line after this line before the next `##` section header
- URL is the `source_url` field from the Article Envelope — never fabricated

Source: CONTEXT.md §文章正文

## Canonical Example Output

The following is a complete, correctly formatted `content/YYYY-MM-DD.md` file matching
every rule in this contract. Executor must produce output structurally identical to this.

```markdown
## 📖 文章 / Article

Scientists have discovered a new species of deep-sea fish near the Mariana Trench. The
creature, which lives at depths of over 8,000 meters, has adapted to survive extreme
pressure and near-zero temperatures. Researchers from three countries collaborated on
the expedition.

The fish uses bioluminescence to attract prey in the dark environment. This is the
first time such a large specimen has been captured on camera.

> Source: https://www.newsinlevels.com/example-article

## 📚 词汇 / Vocabulary

**species** (noun) （物种）— A group of animals or plants that are similar and can breed together.
> "Scientists have discovered a new species of deep-sea fish near the Mariana Trench."

**adapted** (verb) （适应）— Changed over time to survive in a particular environment.
> "The creature has adapted to survive extreme pressure and near-zero temperatures."

**collaborated** (verb) （合作）— Worked together with other people to achieve something.
> "Researchers from three countries collaborated on the expedition."

**bioluminescence** (noun) （生物发光）— The ability of a living thing to produce its own light.
> "The fish uses bioluminescence to attract prey in the dark environment."

**specimen** (noun) （标本/样本）— A single example of something, especially an animal or plant used for study.
> "This is the first time such a large specimen has been captured on camera."

## 🔗 表达 / Chunking Expressions

**near-zero** （接近零度的）
Used to describe temperatures or values that are very close to zero without being exactly zero. Common in scientific and weather contexts.
- The spacecraft was exposed to near-zero temperatures during its orbit around the moon.
- Near-zero interest rates made borrowing money very cheap for several years.

**capture on camera** （用摄像机捕捉到）
A fixed phrase meaning to successfully record something with a camera, especially something rare or difficult to film.
- The wildlife photographer managed to capture the rare snow leopard on camera.
- The security system captured the incident on camera, which helped the investigation.

**collaborate on** （在…上合作）
To work with others on a specific project or task. Followed by a noun phrase describing the shared effort.
- The two universities agreed to collaborate on a climate change study.
- Several governments plan to collaborate on new space exploration missions.

## ❓ 理解 / Comprehension

**Q: Where was the new species of fish discovered?**
**A (EN):** It was discovered near the Mariana Trench, at depths of over 8,000 meters.
**A (中):** 在马里亚纳海沟附近发现，生活在超过8000米深的海底。

**Q: How does the fish find food in its dark environment?**
**A (EN):** The fish uses bioluminescence — its own light — to attract prey.
**A (中):** 鱼利用生物发光能力产生自己的光来吸引猎物。

**Q: Why do you think researchers from three countries worked together on this expedition instead of just one country doing it alone?**
**A (EN):** Deep-sea research requires expensive equipment, specialized expertise, and significant funding. Collaboration allows countries to share these costs and combine different scientific knowledge, making a difficult mission more achievable.
**A (中):** 深海研究需要昂贵的设备和专业知识，多国合作可以共担费用、集合不同领域的专长，使这项艰难的任务更容易实现。
```
