## 📖 文章 / Article

10-04-2026 15:00

The shoe business is changing. Nike, the biggest shoe brand, is having a hard time.

For a few years, Nike didn’t create many new styles, so shoppers started buying shoes from other brands like Hoka and On. Now, Nike has a new boss who wants to make the brand special again.

At the same time, Crocs are popular again. Many people thought they were ugly, but nowadays, you can decorate them with small charms. This helped them sell billions of dollars of shoes. A Swiss brand called On is also becoming a leader. Their shoes look very different and are popular with people who run. On is growing much faster than older brands.

But there is a risk for the future. Many shoes come from Vietnam, and new taxes may make the price of shoes go up. Brands must find ways to stay popular even when prices are rising.

Difficult words: brand (the name and image of a company), ugly (not pleasant to look at, not beautiful), charm (a small, decorative object).

## 🔗 表达 / Chunking Expressions

**having a hard time** （处境困难）
Used to describe a period when someone or something is experiencing difficulties or problems. It's a common and informal phrase.
- The small business is having a hard time surviving the economic downturn.
- She's been having a hard time at work lately due to the heavy workload.

**make the brand special again** （让品牌再次特别）
This phrase refers to the effort to restore or improve the unique identity and appeal of a company's brand, often after a period of decline or neglect. It implies a strategic effort to regain distinction and consumer interest.
- The new CEO's goal is to make the brand special again by focusing on innovative designs.
- They hope that the rebranding campaign will make the brand special again in the eyes of younger consumers.

**popular with** （受...欢迎）
Indicates that something is liked or admired by a particular group of people. It's a very common collocation.
- These new sneakers are very popular with teenagers.
- His latest book is proving popular with critics and readers alike.

**stay popular** （保持受欢迎）
Means to continue to be liked or admired by many people over time. It suggests an ongoing effort to maintain a positive image or appeal.
- The restaurant tries to stay popular by offering new dishes regularly.
- It's a challenge for many artists to stay popular after their initial success.
