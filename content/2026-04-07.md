## 🔗 表达 / Chunking Expressions

**ride bikes** （骑自行车）
A common phrase used to describe the action of using a bicycle for transportation or recreation. It's a very common and neutral expression.
- Many people in my city ride bikes to work.
- She loves to ride bikes in the countryside during the summer.

**break a rule** （违反规则）
To disobey or fail to follow a law, regulation, or principle. This is a standard phrase used in formal and informal contexts when talking about rules and consequences.
- If you break a rule, there will be a penalty.
- It's important not to break a rule when you are driving.

**pay money** （付钱）
To give money in exchange for something or as a penalty. This is a very direct and common phrase.
- You have to pay money if you park illegally.
- Customers pay money for goods and services.

**follow the signs** （遵守指示牌）
To obey the instructions or information given on signs, especially in public places like roads or parks. This implies paying attention and acting according to the displayed guidance.
- Drivers must follow the signs to avoid traffic jams.
- Pedestrians should follow the signs for designated walking paths.
