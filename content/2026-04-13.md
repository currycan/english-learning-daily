## 📖 文章 / Article

10-04-2026 15:00

The shoe business is changing. Nike, the biggest shoe brand, is having a hard time.

For a few years, Nike didn’t create many new styles, so shoppers started buying shoes from other brands like Hoka and On. Now, Nike has a new boss who wants to make the brand special again.

At the same time, Crocs are popular again. Many people thought they were ugly, but nowadays, you can decorate them with small charms. This helped them sell billions of dollars of shoes. A Swiss brand called On is also becoming a leader. Their shoes look very different and are popular with people who run. On is growing much faster than older brands.

But there is a risk for the future. Many shoes come from Vietnam, and new taxes may make the price of shoes go up. Brands must find ways to stay popular even when prices are rising.

Difficult words: brand (the name and image of a company), ugly (not pleasant to look at, not beautiful), charm (a small, decorative object).

## 🔗 表达 / Chunking Expressions

**having a hard time** （处境艰难）
This idiom means experiencing difficulties or problems.
- The company is having a hard time with its new product launch.
- Many small businesses are having a hard time surviving the economic downturn.

**make the brand special again** （让品牌再次变得特别）
This phrase refers to efforts to restore or improve the unique appeal and positive image of a company's brand.
- The new CEO is determined to make the brand special again by focusing on quality.
- They are redesigning their packaging to make the brand special again for younger consumers.

**popular with** （受...欢迎）
This means that many people like or approve of something or someone.
- The new cafe is very popular with students.
- His latest book is proving to be incredibly popular with critics.

**stay popular** （保持受欢迎）
To continue to be liked or admired by a large number of people.
- It's difficult for artists to stay popular for decades.
- The restaurant has managed to stay popular by constantly updating its menu.
