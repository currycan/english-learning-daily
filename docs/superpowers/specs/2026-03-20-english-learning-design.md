# English Learning Plan — Design Spec

**Date:** 2026-03-20
**Author:** Andrew
**Goal:** Systematic English improvement for workplace and daily conversation

---

## Background

- **Current level:** Intermediate — can read most articles, listening is functional, speaking is the primary weak point
- **Goals:** Workplace English (emails, meetings, presentations) + natural daily conversation
- **Daily time budget:** 35–45 minutes, primarily fragmented (commute, waiting, etc.)
- **Preferred input:** Podcasts/audio + written articles

---

## Core Philosophy

**Scene-driven × Authentic Input × Spoken Output**

Every two weeks focuses on one real-world scene. Authentic podcasts and articles supply natural input to extract idiomatic expressions from; AI conversation practice and self-talk convert that input into active spoken output.

Core loop: **Listen/Read authentic material → Extract idiomatic expressions → Scene-based practice → AI dialogue output**

---

## Before You Begin (Week 0 Setup)

Complete these once before starting Week 1:

1. **Choose your expression library tool:** Use Anki (recommended — spaced repetition built-in) or Notion. Set up your entry template: `Expression → Scene → Source sentence → My own example sentence`
2. **Bookmark your input sources:** Subscribe to the three recommended podcasts. Bookmark the written sources.
3. **Record a baseline:** Speak for 2–3 minutes on this topic: *"Introduce yourself and describe your job."* Save the file. This is your Week 0 baseline for comparison.
4. **Schedule your weekly recording block:** Pick one fixed time slot per week (20–30 min uninterrupted) — weekday lunch, weekend morning, etc. Put it in your calendar.

## Daily Structure (Fragmented, 35–45 min total)

Four small blocks — no need to be consecutive:

| Block | Duration | Content |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **Review** | 5 min | Open Anki and review due expression cards. Do not add new cards here — review only. |
| **Input** | 15 min | Listen to a podcast segment related to this week's scene. Select 1–2 minutes of natural dialogue. Play it 3 times: once for meaning, twice while shadowing the speaker's rhythm and intonation. |
| **Extraction** | 10 min | Read one relevant English article (email template / Reddit / Medium). Highlight 3–5 idiomatic expressions. Add each one to your expression library with a source sentence and your own example. |
| **Output** | 10–15 min | Use the scene prompt below to simulate this week's scene with Claude or ChatGPT, or self-talk in English recapping the day using 2–3 expressions from this week. |

**If you miss a day:** Skip it and resume the next day. Do not double up.

**Anki deck size:** Cap your active deck at 150 cards. When you exceed this, archive the oldest 20 cards (mark as "suspended") rather than adding new ones until the deck drops below 100.

---

### Output Block: AI Scene Simulation

Use this prompt format with Claude or ChatGPT:

> "Let's practice English conversation. The scene is: [this week's scene]. You play the role of [a colleague / a new acquaintance / etc.]. Start the conversation naturally. After we finish, give me 3 suggestions for more natural or idiomatic phrasing."

Example for Week 1 (Self-introduction & small talk):
> "Let's practice English conversation. The scene is meeting a new colleague on the first day at work. You play the role of a friendly coworker. Start the conversation naturally. After we finish, give me 3 suggestions for more natural or idiomatic phrasing."

---

## Weekly Recording Block (20–30 min, uninterrupted)

Once per week, at your pre-scheduled time:

1. **Cold attempt (at the start of each new scene cycle):** Before studying, record yourself attempting the scene with no preparation. This is your cycle-start baseline.
2. **Reviewed attempt (at the end of each two-week scene cycle):** Record again using expressions learned during the two weeks.
3. Listen to both recordings back-to-back. Note 2–3 specific improvements and 1–2 areas still weak.

## Expression Library

Build a personal library of **real expressions encountered in context** — not word lists.

- **Recommended tool:** Anki (spaced repetition handles review scheduling automatically)
- **Entry format:** `Expression → Scene → Source sentence → My own example sentence`
- **Daily review:** 5 minutes in the Review block each day
- **Deck size cap:** Keep active deck under 150 cards; suspend oldest cards when over limit

---

## Biweekly Check-in

At the end of each two-week scene cycle:

1. Rate each of the 8 scenes (1–5) on the self-diagnostic below
2. Listen to the cold attempt vs. reviewed attempt recordings for this cycle
3. Decide: advance to next scene, or spend one more week reinforcing

**Self-diagnostic (rate 1–5):**

| Scene | Can I do this without thinking? |
|-------|---------------------------------|
| Self-introduction & small talk | |

| Making plans & social conversation | |

| Expressing emotions & storytelling | |
| Asking questions & confirming information | |

For Weeks 15–16: use this diagnostic to pick the 2–3 lowest-scoring scenes and repeat their structure.

---

## Success Criteria

- Can introduce yourself and small-talk naturally with a native speaker (Weeks 1–2)
- Can write a clear, professional email without relying on translation (Weeks 5–6)
- Can participate in a meeting in English — express opinions, ask questions, give updates (Weeks 9–10)
- After 16 weeks: noticeable improvement in spoken fluency and authenticity as measured by comparing Week 0 baseline to current recordings

---

## What This Plan Does NOT Cover

- Grammar drilling (intermediate learners benefit more from exposure than explicit grammar study)
- Standardized test preparation
- Vocabulary lists (replaced by context-based expression library)
