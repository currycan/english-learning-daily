Surfing is a popular sport in Australia. Every year, it brings about 2 billion dollars to the country’s economy.

Surf breaks are the places where the waves form. These areas are in danger. Things like climate change and problems with the coast are bad for these areas. Countries like New Zealand and Peru have rules. They look after the surf breaks. But Australia needs to do more.

Surfing is important because it helps the economy. It makes people happy, too. Many people, from beginners to experts, love to surf. Businesses near the beach make money from it. Surfing is special because it brings people closer to nature.

## 🔗 表达 / Chunking Expressions

**brings about** （引起；导致）
To cause something to happen. Used in formal and informal contexts.
- The new policy will bring about significant changes in the education system.
- His actions unfortunately brought about a lot of trouble.

**in danger** （处于危险之中）
A situation where something is at risk of harm or loss.  Commonly used.
- The rare animal is in danger of becoming extinct.
- Many coastal areas are in danger from rising sea levels.

**look after** （照顾；照看；保护）
To take care of someone or something. Can be used for people, pets, or even places/things.
- We need to look after our environment for future generations.
- Can you look after my cat while I'm on vacation?

**closer to nature** （更接近大自然）
To feel more connected to the natural world, often through activities outdoors.  Often used to describe a feeling or experience.
- Camping in the mountains always makes me feel closer to nature.
- Living in the countryside can help people feel closer to nature.
