# English Learning Plan — Design Spec

**Date:** 2026-03-20
**Author:** Andrew
**Goal:** Systematic English improvement for workplace and daily conversation

---

## Background

- **Current level:** Intermediate — can read most articles, listening is functional, speaking is the primary weak point
- **Goals:** Workplace English (emails, meetings, presentations) + natural daily conversation
- **Daily time budget:** 30–60 minutes, primarily fragmented (commute, waiting, etc.)
- **Preferred input:** Podcasts/audio + written articles

---

## Core Philosophy

**Scene-driven × Authentic Input × Spoken Output**

Every two weeks focuses on one real-world scene. Authentic podcasts and articles supply natural input to extract idiomatic expressions from; AI conversation practice and self-talk convert that input into active spoken output.

Core loop: **Listen/Read authentic material → Extract idiomatic expressions → Scene-based practice → AI dialogue output**

---

## Scene Roadmap (16 Weeks, 8 Scenes)

| Weeks | Scene | Domain |
|-------|-------|--------|
| 1–2   | Self-introduction & small talk | Both |
| 3–4   | Expressing opinions & discussing solutions | Workplace |
| 5–6   | Email writing & professional written expression | Workplace |
| 7–8   | Making plans & social conversation | Daily |
| 9–10  | Reporting progress & giving feedback | Workplace |
| 11–12 | Expressing emotions & storytelling | Daily |
| 13–14 | Asking questions & confirming information | Both |
| 15–16 | Free review & weak scene reinforcement | Both |

---

## Daily Structure (Fragmented, 30–45 min total)

Three small blocks — no need to be consecutive:

| Block | Duration | Content |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **Input** | 15 min | Listen to a podcast segment related to this week's scene. Pick 1–2 minutes of dense dialogue, repeat and shadow the intonation and rhythm. |
| **Extraction** | 10 min | Read one relevant English article (email template / Reddit / Medium). Highlight 3–5 idiomatic expressions. Add to expression library. |
| **Output** | 10–15 min | Simulate this week's scene with Claude/ChatGPT, or self-talk in English recapping the day. |

**Weekly addition (one 20–30 min uninterrupted block):**
Record a 2–3 minute spoken response using expressions learned this week. Listen back and note issues.

## Expression Library

Build a personal library of **real expressions encountered in context** — not word lists.

- **Tool:** Anki or Notion
- **Entry format:** `Expression → Scene → Source sentence → My own example sentence`
- **Review:** 5 minutes before the Input block each day (Anki spaced repetition handles scheduling automatically)

---

## Biweekly Check-in

At the end of each two-week scene cycle:

1. Compare the new voice recording to the one from the start of the cycle
2. Rate the scene (1–5): how comfortable do you feel now?
3. Decide: advance to next scene, or spend one more week reinforcing

---

## Success Criteria

- Can introduce yourself and small-talk naturally with a native speaker (Weeks 1–2)
- Can write a clear, professional email without relying on translation (Weeks 5–6)
- Can participate in a meeting in English — express opinions, ask questions, give updates (Weeks 9–10)
- After 16 weeks: noticeable improvement in spoken fluency and authenticity as measured by self-recordings

---

## What This Plan Does NOT Cover

- Grammar drilling (intermediate learners benefit more from exposure than explicit grammar study)
- Standardized test preparation
- Vocabulary lists (replaced by context-based expression library)
