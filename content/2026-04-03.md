## 📖 文章 / Article

02-04-2026 15:00

Tracking children’s locations and monitoring their online activity has become more common among parents, raising questions about its effects on children’s independence.

While some kids feel safer knowing someone is watching, others feel restricted, especially when parental controls limit their use of certain apps and websites. Adolescents often find ways to bypass these controls, sometimes making them less safe as they hide their activities from their parents. Experts say that strict monitoring can damage trust and increase rebellious behavior. Parents are advised to have open conversations with their children about technology instead of only relying on controls. By teaching self-regulation and smart decision-making, parents can help kids navigate the digital world responsibly. Some teens believe that by the age of 16, they should have the right to decide whether they are tracked.

Difficult words: bypass (to find a way around), rebellious (acting against control), tracked (followed).

## 🔗 表达 / Chunking Expressions

**raising questions about** （引起关于……的疑问）
Used to introduce a topic that causes doubt or uncertainty, often in formal or semi-formal contexts.
- The new policy is raising questions about its fairness.
- His sudden resignation is raising questions about his future plans.

**find ways to bypass** （找到绕过……的方法）
Describes the action of discovering or creating methods to get around restrictions or rules, often implying cleverness or a need to overcome obstacles.
- Students often find ways to bypass the school's dress code.
- Hackers are constantly trying to find ways to bypass security systems.

**damage trust** （损害信任）
To harm or weaken the belief and confidence that someone has in another person or thing. It suggests a negative impact on a relationship.
- Lying to your friends can damage trust between you.
- The company's unethical practices damaged trust with its customers.

**relying on** （依赖于）
To depend on someone or something for help, support, or success. It implies a need for assistance or a lack of self-sufficiency.
- Instead of relying on luck, she studied hard for the exam.
- Many small businesses are relying on online sales to survive.
